{\question{A javelin thrower exerts a force on the javelin of \quantity{100}{N} for \quantity{0.3}{s}. A javelin weighs \quantity{800}{g}. What is the rate of change of the momentum of the javelin?}
\begin{enumerate}
	\item \choice[a]{\quantity{416.7}{kg\,m\,s\sup{-2}}}
	\item \choice[b]{\quantity{333}{kg\,m\,s\sup{-2}}}
	\item \choice[c]{\quantity{100}{kg\,m\,s\sup{-2}}}\correct
	\item \choice[d]{\quantity{30}{kg\,m\,s\sup{-2}}}
	\item \choice[e]{\quantity{24}{kg\,m\,s\sup{-2}}}
\end{enumerate}
}
%{\textit{Used with permission from UCLES, A Level Physics, June 1989, Paper 1, Question 1.}}
{\stress{Adapted with permission from UCLES, A Level Physics, June 1989, Paper 1, Question 1.}}
{\answer{The correct answer is (c). This can come from \valuedef{F}{\frac{\d p}{\d t}}{} or from working it out long hand using SUVAT, and dividing the change of momentum by the time interval.}
\end{problem}
